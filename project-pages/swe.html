SWE @ University of Utah

The Society of Women Engineers (SWE) is a global non-profit organization dedicated to education and service, playing a pivotal role in promoting engineering as an attractive career option for women.

I redesigned the website to improve the user experience for both SWE members and sponsors and make the process of getting involved with the organization easier. The goal is to ensure that all the information that a user would need to be involved with the community is easily accessible through easy navigation and updates regarding events and other information.

Problem Statement

  • Repetitive Information Clutters Pages

    • Unnecessary repetition of information across pages results in cluttered content

  • Confusing Newsletter Tab

    • While the tab reads 'Newsletter', the tab opens to both Newsletters and Spotlights

  • Limited Event Information

    • Event information is hard to read due to lengthy text and does not follow a clear structure, making it difficult for users to quickly find information

  • Unorganized Sponsorship Information

    • Information regarding becoming a sponsor is not easily accessible or understood

  • Ineffective space usage

    • Current layout has a vertical footer, taking away space for content

  • Important details for committees are unclear

    • The direct responsibilities and members of each committee are unclear

Design Solution

  • Create a simplified userflow across the entire site

  • Prioritize the promotion of events by narrowing the Newsletter Tab content scope to just newsletters

  • Highlight the purpose, location, time, and guests of each event to simplify event information

  • Provide users a list of events categorized by group for easy navigation

  • Maximize the website width by adding the footer to the bottom rather than the side

  • Highlight committee responsibilities through descriptions and images

Competitor Analysis

We analyzed the features that various SWE websites contained across colleges across the country. From this analysis, we realize that SWE at the University of Utah has a higher need to make event information accessible as they often plan and host events within a shorter timeframe than SWE at other campuses.

Furthermore, SWE at the U heavily wants to emphasize ways to get involved with the organization, which makes the accessibility of contact, event, and sponsorship information high priority.
